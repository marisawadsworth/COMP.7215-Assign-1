CMS Installation


Joomla

For this assignment, I used cPanel provided by Toi Ohomai to download Joomla. It was fast and easy to install. Joomla was written in PHP.
The content for the website is stored in the cPanel database.

There were no themes. It was difficult to use and find my way around the website. I needed to use learning resources before I could start creating a website. The format was strange. When you edit a page, the content is everywhere and not structured. If you're copying and pasting a text from somewhere else, you will need to re-format it on the page.

To add plug-ins you click Extensions, then Plugins. There is a range of plug-ins, all you need to do is enable the plug-in by pressing the red cross to a green tick by its name, you can also click on the name of the plug-in, it will take you to its setting where you can configure it.
The modules page shows a list of features on your website and where they are placed.

Google Sites

I used Google Sites as the other CMS. There was no installation for this CMS as everything was online. Google was written in Java and Python.
The content for the website is stored in your Google Drive.

It was easy to find a theme. There are three tabs at the top saying “Insert”, “Pages”, and “Themes” all of them are free. Click on the theme you want, and it loads onto the screen.

To find the plug-ins go to the insert page and scroll to the bottom, most of the plug-ins are from Google itself with Google docs, slides, sheets, forms, charts. The others are embedded by YouTube, Maps etc. It was easy to use these plug-ins. You just click on the plug-in you want, and it asks you to select a file from your Google Drive to add to the website, or to add a link.

Google Sites is just a drag and drop what you want onto your website. It was simple to use.

(You need to be signed into your Toi Ohomai Google Email to see the website).
