Joomla Google Sites
Overview Joomla is a free open source CMS developed by Open Source Matters, Inc. It is built on a Model-View-Controller (MVC) application framework.
Joomla is written in PHP, and stores data in a MySQL database.		 Google Sites is a free CMS developed by Google.
To use Google Sites, you need a Gmail Account.
It is a structured wiki and web-page tool.
History Joomla was first released on the 17th of August 2005. Joomla was the result of a fork of Mambo when it was released. The Mambo name was a trademark of Mico International Pvt. Ltd, who formed a non-profit foundation.
On September 22, the name was changed to Joomla after developers created a website called OpenSourceMatters.org to distribute information to the software community. 		Google Sites was first released on the 28th of February 2008.
Google Sites started as JotSpot, the name of a software company that offers enterprise software.
It was founded by Joe Kraus and Graham Spencer, co-founders of Excite.
Features
  • Offered in 75 languages.
  • SEO
  • Free
  • Can create different types of websites including, blogs, business websites, community websites etc.
  • Has extensions to provide functionality.
  • User management. Controls users.
  • Creates menu items.
  • Cache management for performance.
  • Responsive design
  • Drag and drop editing
  • Levels of permissions (Owner, Editor and Viewer).
  • Integration with Google Drive, Google Maps, Google Forms, etc.
Capabilities and uses Joomla requires you to have a basic understanding of technical skills. You also need basic HTML and CSS skills. You don’t need skills to work Google Sites, it’s all just editing and adding/drag and drop.
Cost Free Free
What its used for? Joomla can be used for both small and large websites. It is used for e-commerce, social networking, blogs, and community websites. Google Sites are used for a small basic website. It's designed for people to create an online CV, a basic blog, advertising, etc.
Business problems that it solves Joomla is great for government and organizational websites. Great for small businesses who don’t need a big website.
